# Crew Travel Benefits Policy

## Standby Travel Privileges

### Eligibility
- All active crew members after 6 months of service
- Immediate family members (spouse, dependent children)
- Registered domestic partners
- Parents (limited passes per year)

### Priority Levels
1. **SA1**: Crew on duty travel
2. **SA2**: Personal travel - active crew
3. **SA3**: Retirees with 25+ years
4. **SA4**: Family and companions
5. **SA5**: Buddy passes

### Booking Process
- Book through employee travel portal
- List up to 24 hours before departure
- Check loads before heading to airport
- Dress code applies for cabin travel

## Positive Space Travel

### When Available
- Crew positioning (deadhead)
- Training travel
- Company meetings
- Emergency situations approved by management

### Booking
- Booked by crew scheduling
- Confirmed seat assignment
- No dress code restrictions

## Interline Agreements

### ZED Fares
- Reduced fares on partner airlines
- Must purchase through myIDTravel
- Subject to availability
- Valid airlines: Delta, United, Southwest, JetBlue, Alaska

### OAL Standby
- Standby on some partner airlines
- Check individual airline policies
- ID90 travel portal for bookings

## Companion Passes

### Annual Allocation
- 8 companion passes per year
- Additional passes for 10+ years service
- Cannot be sold or transferred

### Eligible Companions
- Friends and extended family
- Must register in system
- Background check required

## International Travel

### Requirements
- Valid passport (6+ months validity)
- Appropriate visas
- Vaccination requirements
- Customs declaration

### Benefits
- Same standby privileges apply
- Premium cabin upgrades when available
- Lounge access with priority status

## Vacation Travel

### Peak Period Restrictions
- Blackout dates during holidays
- Reduced priority during peak travel
- Book early for better chances

### Tips for Success
- Check flight loads frequently
- Have backup plans
- Travel light for quick boarding
- Arrive early at airport

## Uniform Requirements

### When Required
- Always in cockpit/cabin jumpseat
- Main cabin in uniform or business casual
- Premium cabins: business attire

### When Not Required
- Positive space personal travel
- Travel in cargo hold (not applicable)

## Lost Luggage

### Crew Bags
- Company policy covers essential items
- Report immediately to baggage services
- Keep receipts for reimbursement

### Personal Travel
- Standard airline policies apply
- Travel insurance recommended

## Retiree Benefits

### Eligibility
- 10+ years of service
- Age 55+ or medical retirement
- Lifetime benefits for 25+ years

### Limitations
- Lower priority than active crew
- Some international restrictions
- Annual pass limitations

## Taxes and Fees

### Employee Responsibility
- Imputed income tax on travel benefits
- Reported on W-2
- Segment fees may apply

### Company Paid
- Fuel surcharges
- Facility charges
- Security fees
