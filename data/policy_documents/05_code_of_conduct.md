# Crew Code of Conduct and Professional Standards

## Alcohol Policy
- **Bottle-to-throttle rule for pilots**: Minimum 8 hours
- **Bottle-to-throttle rule for cabin crew**: Minimum 12 hours
- **Maximum BAC on duty**: 0.04%
- Random and post-incident testing applies
- Violation results in immediate removal from duty

## Drug and Substance Policy
- Zero tolerance for illegal drugs
- Random drug testing program in effect
- Prescription medications must be approved by company medical
- Some over-the-counter medications are restricted
- Positive test results in immediate termination

## Professional Appearance
- Uniform must be clean, pressed, and complete
- Name badge visible at all times
- Hair must be neat and professional
- Visible tattoos must be covered
- Jewelry limited to wedding ring and small earrings
- Fragrances should be subtle

## Punctuality
- Report on time for all duty assignments
- Late report is a serious offense
- Three late reports in 12 months triggers review
- No-show without notification: subject to termination

## Customer Service Standards
- Greet all passengers professionally
- Address concerns promptly and courteously
- Maintain calm demeanor in difficult situations
- Report service issues through proper channels
- Never argue with passengers

## Social Media Policy
- Do not post in uniform without authorization
- Do not disclose confidential company information
- Do not make statements on behalf of the company
- Personal posts should not damage company reputation
- Report any harassment or threats to security

## Confidentiality
- Passenger information is strictly confidential
- Flight operational details are confidential
- Company business strategies are confidential
- Violation may result in termination and legal action

## Anti-Harassment Policy
- Zero tolerance for harassment of any kind
- Includes verbal, physical, and sexual harassment
- Report incidents to HR or anonymous hotline
- Retaliation against reporters is prohibited
- Investigations are conducted confidentially

## Conflict of Interest
- Disclose any outside employment
- Do not accept gifts over $50 from vendors
- Do not use company resources for personal gain
- Report potential conflicts to management

## Reporting Misconduct
- Use anonymous hotline: 1-800-AA-ETHICS
- Report to direct supervisor or HR
- Whistleblower protections in effect
- Good faith reports will not result in retaliation

## Disciplinary Process
1. **Verbal Warning**: Documented conversation
2. **Written Warning**: Formal documentation in file
3. **Final Warning**: Last chance before termination
4. **Suspension**: Temporary removal from duty
5. **Termination**: End of employment

## Serious Misconduct
Immediate termination may result from:
- Falsification of records
- Theft
- Violence or threats
- Intoxication on duty
- Gross insubordination
- Safety violations
