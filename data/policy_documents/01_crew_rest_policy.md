# Crew Rest Policy and Minimum Rest Requirements

## Overview
This policy outlines American Airlines' crew rest requirements in accordance with FAR Part 117 regulations. All flight crew members must comply with these rest requirements to ensure safe flight operations.

## Minimum Rest Period
- The minimum rest period before a flight duty period is **10 consecutive hours**.
- This rest period must include an **8-hour sleep opportunity**.
- The sleep opportunity must be uninterrupted and in suitable accommodations.

## Reduced Rest
- Reduced rest (minimum 8 hours) may be used under specific conditions:
  - Both the company and the crew member must agree to the reduced rest.
  - Reduced rest can only be used once between periods of 30 consecutive hours free from duty.
  - The subsequent rest period must be at least 14 hours.

## Rest Facility Classifications
Rest facilities are classified as follows:

### Class 1 (Bunk)
- A bunk or berth in an isolated area separate from the flight deck and passenger cabin.
- Temperature and light control.
- Maximum FDP extension: 3 hours for 3-pilot crew.

### Class 2 (Flat Seat)
- A seat that reclines at least 40 degrees from vertical.
- Leg rest and foot support.
- Located in an isolated area.
- Maximum FDP extension: 2 hours for 3-pilot crew.

### Class 3 (Reclining Seat)
- A seat that reclines at least 20 degrees from vertical.
- Leg rest and foot support.
- Separated from passengers by a curtain or partition.
- Maximum FDP extension: 1.5 hours for 3-pilot crew.

## Weekly Rest Requirements
- Every crew member must have at least **30 consecutive hours** free from all duty within any **168-hour window**.
- This includes time for travel to and from duty.

## Rest After International Operations
- After crossing more than **60 degrees longitude**, crew members are entitled to:
  - 56 consecutive hours of rest, or
  - 3 consecutive local nights of rest (whichever is longer).

## Layover Rest
- Layover rest begins when the crew member arrives at the hotel.
- Transportation time from airport to hotel is NOT counted as rest.
- Minimum layover rest: 10 hours, including 8-hour sleep opportunity.

## Crew Responsibilities
- Crew members must report for duty well-rested and fit to fly.
- If a crew member is too fatigued to safely perform duties, they must notify the company.
- Fatigued crew members will not face punishment for reporting fatigue concerns.

## Hotel Standards
- All layover hotels must meet company standards for:
  - Quiet rooms away from elevators and ice machines
  - Blackout curtains
  - Temperature control
  - Do-not-disturb policies for crew
