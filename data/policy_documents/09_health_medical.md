# Health and Medical Policy

## Medical Certification Requirements

### Pilots
- **First Class Medical**: Required for ATP privileges
  - Valid 12 months (under 40) / 6 months (40+)
  - FAA-designated Aviation Medical Examiner (AME)
  - Includes vision, hearing, cardiovascular, neurological

- **Second Class Medical**: Required for commercial privileges
  - Valid 12 months
  - Less stringent cardiovascular requirements

### Flight Attendants
- No FAA medical certificate required
- Company fitness-for-duty standards apply
- Annual health screening recommended
- Hearing and vision standards must be met

## Fit-to-Fly Standards

### Self-Assessment Before Duty
Ask yourself:
1. Am I free from illness or infection?
2. Have I had adequate rest?
3. Am I free from the effects of alcohol?
4. Am I free from impairing medications?
5. Do I have any new medical conditions?

### Conditions Requiring Grounding
- Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
- Vomiting or diarrhea
- Severe cold or flu symptoms
- Ear or sinus infection
- Recent surgery (varies by type)
- New prescription medication

### Medication Restrictions
**Never Acceptable:**
- Sedatives
- Narcotic pain medications
- Anti-anxiety medications
- Most antihistamines (first generation)

**May Be Acceptable (with approval):**
- Second-generation antihistamines
- Some blood pressure medications
- Thyroid medications
- Birth control

**Always Report:**
- Any new medication
- Changes in dosage
- Over-the-counter medications

## Reporting Medical Conditions

### Immediate Reporting Required
- Loss of consciousness
- Heart attack or stroke
- Seizure
- Diabetes diagnosis
- Mental health diagnosis
- DUI/DWI arrest
- Failed drug/alcohol test

### Within 30 Days
- New chronic condition diagnosis
- Surgery
- Hospitalization
- Significant injury

### How to Report
1. Contact company medical department
2. Email: crewmedical@aa.com
3. Phone: 1-800-555-MEDC
4. Provide physician documentation

## Return to Duty Process

### After Illness
- Symptom-free for 24 hours minimum
- No impairing medications
- Self-assess fitness
- Contact scheduling

### After Surgery
- Medical clearance from treating physician
- Company medical review
- May require fitness evaluation
- Graduated return for some procedures

### After Pregnancy
- 6 weeks postpartum (vaginal delivery)
- 8 weeks postpartum (cesarean)
- Medical clearance required
- Lactation accommodations available

## Pregnancy Policy

### Flight Duties
- May continue until 32 weeks (domestic)
- May continue until 28 weeks (international)
- Ground duty available after cutoff
- Full pay and benefits maintained

### Accommodations
- No heavy lifting
- Additional rest breaks
- Priority for preferred schedules
- Comfortable seating

### Leave
- 12 weeks maternity leave
- FMLA protection
- Short-term disability available
- Return to same position guaranteed

## Occupational Health

### Common Occupational Hazards
- Cosmic radiation exposure
- Circadian rhythm disruption
- Cabin air quality
- Noise exposure
- Musculoskeletal strain

### Mitigation Measures
- Radiation monitoring for frequent flyers
- Fatigue management program
- HEPA filtration on aircraft
- Hearing protection available
- Ergonomic training

### Work-Related Injury
1. Report to supervisor immediately
2. Seek medical attention
3. File incident report
4. Workers' compensation available
5. Modified duty if applicable

## Mental Health Support

### Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Confidential counseling
- 8 free sessions per issue
- Available 24/7
- Family members eligible

### HIMS Program (Pilots)
- Human Intervention Motivation Study
- Path back to flying after substance issues
- Requires ongoing monitoring
- High success rate

### Peer Support
- Trained peer volunteers
- Confidential conversations
- Available at all bases
- Not a disciplinary resource
